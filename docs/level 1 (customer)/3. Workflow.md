\# Ecommerce Workflows (Customer + Store Operations)



This document explains how the store works day-to-day: what customers do on the storefront, and what your team does in the admin dashboard to fulfill orders.



---



\## 1) Customer Shopping Workflow (Storefront)



1\. Discover products

\- Customer browses categories or uses search.

\- Customer opens a product page to review images, description, price, and available options (size/color).



2\. Choose options and add to cart

\- Customer selects variant options (if applicable) and quantity.

\- Customer adds items to the cart and reviews the cart summary (items, quantities, subtotal).



3\. Checkout

\- Customer enters contact details (email/phone if required).

\- Customer enters shipping address.

\- Customer selects a shipping method (if multiple options are offered).

\- Customer reviews the final total (items + shipping − discounts).



4\. Payment

\- Customer selects a payment method and completes payment.

\- If payment fails, the customer is shown a clear error and can retry or choose a different method.



5\. Confirmation

\- Customer sees an order confirmation page with an order number.

\- Customer receives a confirmation message (typically email) with the order details.



6\. After purchase: tracking and delivery

\- Customer can check order status in their account (if account features are enabled) or via a tracking link.

\- When the order is shipped, the customer receives a notification with tracking information (when available).

\- The order is delivered.



---



\## 2) Store Team Workflow (Admin Dashboard)



1\. Prepare the store (before selling)

\- Create product categories and add products.

\- Add product variants (size/color) where needed.

\- Upload product images and set prices.

\- Configure inventory (stock levels) so the system can prevent overselling.

\- Configure shipping methods (zones, fees, conditions) and payment methods.



2\. Process incoming orders

\- Monitor new orders coming in.

\- Confirm payment status (automatically for online payments, or manually for offline methods if applicable).

\- Verify stock availability and allocate items to the order.



3\. Fulfillment (pick, pack, ship)

\- Pick items from inventory.

\- Pack items, generate packing slip/invoice if required.

\- Create shipment details and tracking number (manual or integrated with a carrier).

\- Update the order to “Shipped” once it leaves the warehouse.



4\. Delivery and completion

\- Update the order to “Delivered” when delivery is confirmed (if your process includes this step).

\- Handle post-delivery support: returns, exchanges, refunds (if in scope for your store).



---



\## 3) Order Status Glossary (Customer-Friendly)



These are typical statuses customers and staff will see. Exact labels can be customized.



\- Cart / In progress: Customer is still shopping and hasn’t completed checkout.

\- Payment pending: Checkout started, but payment is not yet confirmed.

\- Confirmed: Payment is confirmed (or the store has confirmed the order).

\- Processing: Store team is preparing items (picking/packing).

\- Shipped: Package has left the warehouse; tracking may be available.

\- Delivered: Package has arrived (optional step depending on operations).

\- Cancelled: The order will not be fulfilled.

\- Refunded: Payment has been returned (full or partial).



---



\## 4) Common Scenarios \& How We Handle Them



\- Out of stock after purchase (rare): Notify customer, offer substitution, partial fulfillment, or refund (based on your policy).

\- Address change request: Allowed only before shipping; once shipped, changes depend on the carrier.

\- Partial shipment: If items ship from different locations or at different times, the order may be split into multiple shipments.

\- Customer cancellation: Allowed before shipping (rules defined by your policy); after shipping, handled as a return.

\- Failed payment: Customer retries payment, changes method, or the order is cancelled after a timeout window.



---



\## 5) What We Need From You (Configuration Inputs)



To configure a v1 store quickly, we typically need:



\- Brand assets: Logo, colors, typography, product imagery style.

\- Store policies: Returns/exchanges, shipping policy, privacy, terms.

\- Payment method choices: Which gateways/methods you want to support.

\- Shipping setup: Delivery regions, fees, free-shipping thresholds, carrier preferences.

\- Tax rules: How taxes should be calculated (by region, included/excluded in prices).

\- Operational rules: Order cutoff times, packing workflow, SLA targets, support contact.
